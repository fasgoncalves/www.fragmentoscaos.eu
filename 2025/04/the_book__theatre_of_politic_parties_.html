“Theatre of Politic Parties”

Theatre of Parties is not just a political essay — it is an act of civic awakening.

In this book, Francisco Gonçalves unmasks the machinery of the modern party system, where citizens are cast as spectators rather than true participants in their own democracies. Through sharp critique, historical reflection, and philosophical insight, the author reveals the repetitive rituals, symbolic gestures, and media performances that replace genuine representation with political theatre.

More than a denunciation, this is a call to consciousness.
A call to question the legitimacy of a system that has lost contact with the will of the people.
A call to reimagine politics not as a stage managed by elites, but as a shared space for real deliberation and collective authorship.

Theatre of Parties speaks to every citizen who feels that democracy has become an illusion — and who dreams of reclaiming it.
